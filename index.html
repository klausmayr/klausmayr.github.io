I use maps, audio, and a variety of other tools to make information more accessible and collaborative.

For the past three years, I have worked at the Chapel Hill Public Library as part of its Community History team. Currently, my work there involves leading the development of an oral history archive and creating innovative ways to engage the public in community history. (Click to learn more)

Check out some of the projects I have worked on:

  • I was part of the team that started the Re/Collecting Chapel Hill Podcast.. I led production for several of the episodes and provided editing and research support on most of the others.
  • I created videos, did research, and helped design and put together the I Was Still Singing Exhibit. in 2020.
  • I converted a rotary phone replica into a voice recorder and audio player using a Raspberry Pi. When a users picks up the phone, it plays a prompt that we upload to it, records the users message, and then uploads the recording to our Dropbox for easy access and sustainable use.
    Here is a demo and here is the exhibit it is currently being used for.

    The device runs off of a Python code and a few bash scripts (source code). I'm working on making it very easy to replicate so that it can be utilized by other organizations.


In my spare time (also for the last three years), I create innovative digital and physical exhibits with the Carolina Cartography Collective, an interdisciplinary research group consisting of designers, geographers, programmers, and historians.

Along the way, I've also graduated with distinction from UNC-Chapel Hill with a B.A. in Geography; produced audio for NASA, UNC-Chapel Hill, and the public library; taught undergraduate students about ways to study the history of urban environments; and done research at the Instituto Pensar on urban history and environmental justice in Bogotá. Before all of that, I was also a research assistant for a socio-ecologist in South Africa and interned at a start-up in Bogotá that was working to create a more just agricultural supply chain.
